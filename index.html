Our Services

Orthopedic Physiotherapy

Focuses on accurate assessment, diagnosis, and treatment of muscular injuries.

Orthopedic physiotherapy is used to treat conditions affecting the musculoskeletal system, including bones, joints, ligaments, and muscles. It helps in reducing pain, improving strength and flexibility, and restoring movement post-injury or surgery. This therapy is commonly used for fractures, sprains, arthritis, and post-operative rehabilitation. Techniques such as manual therapy, exercises, and electrotherapy are often employed. It aims to prevent further complications and improve overall mobility.

Neurological Physiotherapy

Treats movement and function disorders originating from the nervous system.

Neurological physiotherapy helps individuals with conditions like stroke, Parkinson’s disease, multiple sclerosis, and spinal cord injuries. It focuses on improving balance, coordination, and movement patterns to enhance independence in daily activities. Treatment may include gait training, muscle strengthening, and neuro-rehabilitation techniques. Therapists use targeted exercises to stimulate neural pathways and restore motor function. Regular sessions can significantly improve quality of life and prevent complications like muscle stiffness.

Post Surgical Physiotherapy

Essential for regaining pain-free function after surgery.

Post-surgical physiotherapy is crucial for a faster and safer recovery following procedures like joint replacements, ligament repairs, and spinal surgeries. It involves gentle exercises, pain management strategies, and mobility training to restore normal function. Physiotherapists guide patients through progressive rehabilitation to strengthen muscles and reduce stiffness. Techniques such as ultrasound therapy, manual therapy, and stretching exercises aid in healing. It also helps in reducing post-operative complications and dependency on pain medication.


Sports Physiotherapy

Essential for athletes to recover from injuries, and make them fit and healthy.

Sports physiotherapy is designed for athletes and individuals engaged in physical activities to prevent and treat sports-related injuries. It focuses on improving flexibility, strength, and endurance while reducing the risk of re-injury. Common treatments include taping, dry needling, and exercise therapy to restore optimal function. It plays a vital role in post-injury rehabilitation and enhances performance through specialized training programs. Regular physiotherapy sessions can help athletes maintain peak physical condition.


Respiratory Physiotherapy

Focuses on lung function and breathing techniques.

Respiratory physiotherapy is used to improve lung function and breathing efficiency in individuals with conditions like asthma, COPD, and cystic fibrosis. Techniques such as chest physiotherapy, breathing exercises, and postural drainage help clear mucus and enhance oxygen intake. It is particularly beneficial for post-COVID patients and those recovering from pneumonia. Physiotherapists provide guidance on correct breathing patterns and lifestyle modifications. This therapy plays a crucial role in preventing respiratory complications and improving endurance.

Women Physiotherapy

Specialized care for women's health-related issues.

Women’s physiotherapy focuses on health concerns such as pregnancy-related pain, pelvic floor dysfunction, and postnatal recovery. It helps in managing conditions like urinary incontinence, osteoporosis, and post-menopausal changes. Strengthening exercises, manual therapy, and core stabilization techniques are commonly used. Physiotherapists provide tailored programs for pre and postnatal care to improve strength and mobility. This therapy enhances overall well-being and reduces discomfort associated with hormonal changes.

Gastric Physiotherapy

Exercises to aid in digestive recovery and health.

Gastric physiotherapy is designed to aid digestion and relieve discomfort caused by conditions like acid reflux, bloating, and irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). Specific exercises and abdominal massages help in improving gut motility and reducing gastric distress. Deep breathing and relaxation techniques can also enhance digestion and reduce stress-related gut issues. It is particularly helpful for post-surgical patients recovering from abdominal procedures. This therapy promotes better digestion and overall gastrointestinal health.

Cardio Physiotherapy

Cardio-based exercises for heart and lung health.

Cardio physiotherapy helps patients recovering from heart conditions, lung diseases, and post-cardiac surgeries. It focuses on improving endurance, circulation, and respiratory efficiency through controlled exercise programs. Techniques such as aerobic training, breathing exercises, and resistance workouts strengthen the cardiovascular system. It reduces the risk of heart attacks, strokes, and other cardiovascular issues. Physiotherapists guide patients through progressive training for a healthier heart and improved stamina.


Pre-surgery Physiotherapy

Prepares patients for surgery with tailored exercises.

Pre-surgery physiotherapy, or prehabilitation, helps patients strengthen muscles and improve mobility before undergoing surgery. It enhances recovery rates by preparing the body for post-surgical rehabilitation. Patients undergoing joint replacements, heart surgeries, or orthopedic procedures benefit greatly from prehabilitation. Exercises focus on flexibility, strength, and breathing techniques to ensure a smoother post-surgery experience. It reduces recovery time and improves overall surgical outcomes.
